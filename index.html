Day 2 · What only we can do

The UX moat

"Execution is free. What relies purely on human judgment?"
The question that opened our Day 2 brain drain exercise

We brain-drained, clustered, then drafted proof-of-value statements in pairs. Tap each area to see what we discussed.

Human values and ethical judgment
Empathy and storytelling
Systems thinking
Conceptual discovery
Empathy and storytelling. There was real debate here. Some felt AI will soon be a great storyteller. The counter was that empathetic, inspirational storytelling the kind that gets stakeholders to believe an idea is their own requires human judgment and intent. We landed on: yes, it's a human quality, but UX still has a particular craft in it that others don't.
Systems thinking. This was split into two directions: design system evolution (knowing why past decisions were made, integrating new output into the whole) and conceptual discovery (finding new UX patterns and interactions, pushing the boundaries of what UI can be). Other functions can generate single items via prompts. We integrate them into a coherent system.
Conceptual discovery. "Left-field, illogical, connecting-the-dots" thinking. The kind of exploration that finds new interaction paradigms rather than refining existing ones. AI can generate variations inside a paradigm. Shifting the paradigm itself still requires human imagination and taste.
Synthesis at scale
Strategic framing
Simulation ownership
Synthesis at scale. Cross-study pattern identification, connecting qualitative and quantitative signals, building thematic repositories from years of research. AI can surface patterns; UX decides which ones matter and what they mean for the product.
Strategic framing. Knowing what's worth building. Aligning what we build with values, common sense, and contextual understanding of user history. Not just "what do users want" but "what should we build and why does it matter." That framing is ours.
Synthetic user and market simulation. A future capability we discussed: running designs through AI-generated user models to test concepts before real research. Ownership isn't decided it might sit with commercial or product but the UX lens on what to simulate and how to interpret the results is ours.
Day 2 · Workflow mapping

Mapping the
new workflow

Three disciplines, three distinct AI opportunities. We went deep into each to understand not just what to accelerate, but what to protect.

  • Transcript analysis and theme identificationAutomated tagging and pattern surfacing from research sessions, reducing time on synthesis groundwork.
  • Cross-study pattern miningQuerying across multiple studies to find signals that wouldn't emerge from any single piece of research.
  • Quantitative and survey analysisStatistical and behavioural data processing with AI, reducing manual effort on number-heavy studies.
  • AI-powered recruitment and participant managementCurrently consuming 1.5 headcount worth of effort. Target: cut manual effort by 50% through automated emails, tracking, and voucher sending.
  • Survey translationWhat currently takes up to 6 weeks gets reduced to a reviewer role. Already being tested.
  • Journey mapping automationReducing the manual work of mapping user journeys so more time goes into interpreting them.
  • Global prompt libraryA shared, growing repository of prompts for the team including a Slack-integrated version being rebuilt with Ashwin.
  • AI idea evaluatorOne of our designers built a Claude-powered agent that evaluates ideas across product, user research, business, and design lenses using past PRDs and research insights as context. Exploration time went from 1 hour to 1 day per idea down to 30 to 60 minutes. He generated 40 ideas in two days this way.
  • Synthetic user testingAn emerging concept: running design ideas through AI-generated user models before going to real research. The question we didn't fully resolve is who owns this UX, research, or commercial. But the potential is significant.
  • Agentic prototypingThe core problem: prototypes today are too rigid and limited for interactive testing. The direction we're exploring is AI-generated, Bento-compliant prototypes that can be produced faster and varied more easily so designers stop defaulting to static screens.
  • Figma AI integrationExploring how Figma's emerging AI features can plug into the Bento design system enabling designers to generate compliant layouts and components rather than building from scratch each time.
  • Design QAA CPO-level priority. The team has been mapping the problems to solve. The goal: AI that can check whether what was designed actually matches what was shipped, closing the gap between Figma and live product.
  • Spec generation automationBuilt on Uber's open-source tooling. Automatically generates component specs heights, colour tokens, interaction styles, states that engineers consume and that feed into prototyping pipelines. One of the biggest time savers on the roadmap.
  • Component adoption trackingAn analyzer that checks how much of Bento is being used in a given design file, alongside a live in-app Bento usage tracker. For the first time, we can see the delta between what was designed and what's actually in the product.
  • Token generation and managementThousands of design tokens generated for Spark. An internal Token Lab tool replaced paid Token Studio, saving €1000 annually and 8 hours of manual re-linking work.
  • Component migration toolAI-powered tool that automates swapping legacy components to new Spark components critical given the hundreds of screens that still need updating.
  • Pre-mapping Figma pluginA Figma plugin in development that pre-maps a file with the expected rigor and enabler standards before any design work begins so that the setup is already correct from day one, without needing a mandatory checklist.

Day 3 · Performance

How we think
about performance

Day 3 was less about verdicts and more about direction. Four things came out of the conversation that felt worth anchoring to.

1
Outcomes over outputs
AI fluency shouldn't be measured by how much someone uses the tools. A high performer who delivers exceptional work with or without AI is still a high performer. The question is: what did the work enable, and can we see the impact?
2
Fluency lives inside the craft
Rather than adding "AI fluency" as a standalone skill, we'll embed expectations into existing design enablers: craft, file structure, discovery workflows. If you're using AI well, it should be visible in the work itself.
3
Rigor standards are rising
Across all tracks, expectations are going up around file structure, design QA, Bento component usage, spec quality, and prototyping standards. Leadership will be more rigorous in calibrations. The bar is moving and that's intentional.
4
Curiosity and pace as signals
In a world where tools evolve every few weeks, one of the clearest signals of a strong performer is whether they stay curious and keep pace. Not perfection genuine engagement with what's changing and what it means for the work.
Operational standards
The checklist is getting real
A manifesto and rigor checklist are being packaged for the team, with a target of June. The specifics what rigor means per enabler, how it gets codified into calibrations are being worked through collaboratively.
Ongoing conversation

Two directions we're exploring

This is still a live discussion. We surfaced two meaningful orientations for how roles within UX might develop. Nothing is decided.

  • Research synthesis and insights
  • User knowledge agents
  • Prompt prototyping
  • Qualitative AI-augmented studies
  • Strategic UX framing and problem definition
  • Human-centered evaluation of AI outputs
  • Agentic prototyping and implementation
  • Figma plugin development
  • GitHub code contributions
  • Design system and AI governance
  • Tool building and team enablement
  • Model and data-adjacent UX work

Still in progress. These orientations aren't formal tracks yet. They're a starting frame for a conversation we'll continue with the wider team about how expertise and specialisation can evolve in a way that feels right for everyone.
